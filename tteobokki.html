Tteobokki Recipe

Ttebokki

Description

Ttebokki is one of the most popular dishes in South Korea. It is widely eaten on a daily basis and is the staple of many families. It's made up of long rice cakes called “teok” cooked in a gochujang, a spicy condiment, sauce. There are many different toppings that can be added to this dish to enhance its flavor such as eggs and fish balls.

Ingredients

  • Rice Cakes (1 lb)
  • Water (4 cups)
  • Large Dried Anchovies (7 fishies)
  • Dried Kelp (1 6x8 inch leaf)
  • Gochujang (1/3 cup)
  • Gochugaru (1 tbps)
  • Sugar (1 tbps)
  • Scallions (3, cut into 3 inch long pieces)
  • Hard Boiled Eggs (2 eggs, optional)
  • Fish Cakes (1/2 lb, optional)

Steps

  1. Add water, anchovies, and dried kelp to shallow pot/pan.
  2. Boil for 15 minutes over medium high heat without lid.
  3. Combine Gochujang, Gochugaru, and sugar into a mixture in a bowl.
  4. Remove anchovies and kelp after 15 minutes. Add rice cake, mixture, scallions, and optional fish cakes and hard boiled eggs.
    Note: Stock should be around 2 and 1/2 cups.
  5. Stir gently when it starts to boil, then let simmer and keep stirring until rice cake turns soft and sauce thickens and looks shiny. (Should take around 10-15 minutes)
    Note: If rice cakes not soft enough, add more water and continue stirring until it softens.
    Note 2: If using frozen rice cakes, thaw and soak in cold water to soften before cooking.
  6. Remove from heat and serve hot. If leftover remains, store in fridge and reheat whenever necessary. Finish everything in 3-4 days.
