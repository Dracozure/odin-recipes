Tteobokki Recipe

Tteobokki

Description

Ttebokki is one of the most popular dishes in South Korea. It is widely eaten on a daily basis and is the staple of many families. It's made up of long rice cakes called “tteok” cooked in gochujang sauce, a spicy condiment.
There are many different toppings that can be added to this dish to enhance its flavor such as eggs and fish balls.

Ingredients

  • Tteok Rice Cakes (1 lb)
  • Water (4 cups)
  • Large Dried Anchovies (7 fishies)
  • Dried Kelp (1 6x8 inch leaf)
  • Gochujang (1/3 cup)
  • Gochugaru (1 tbps)
  • Sugar (1 tbps)
  • Green Onions (3, cut into 3 inch long pieces)
  • Hard Boiled Eggs (2 eggs, optional)
  • Fish Cakes (1/2 lb, optional)

Steps

Prep Ingredients

  1. If tteobokki have been in the fridge, soak in cold water for 10 to 15 minutes to soften.
  2. In a bowl, combine gochujang, gochugaru, and sugar into a mixture.

Making The Dish

  1. Add water, anchovies, and dried kelp to shallow pot/pan.
  2. Boil for 15 minutes over medium high heat without lid.
  3. Remove anchovies and kelp after 15 minutes. Add rice cake, mixture, green onions, and optional fish cakes and hard boiled eggs.
    Note: Stock should be around 2 and 1/2 cups.
  4. Stir gently when it starts to boil, then let simmer and keep stirring until rice cake turns soft and sauce thickens and looks shiny (Should take around 10-15 minutes).
    Note: If rice cakes not soft enough, add more water and continue stirring until it softens.
  5. Remove from heat and serve hot. If leftover remains, store in fridge and reheat whenever necessary. Finish everything in 3 to 4 days.
