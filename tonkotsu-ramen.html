Japanese Tonkotsu Ramen Recipe

Japanese Tonkotsu Ramen

Very hard to make, but it's worth the price.

Ingredients

Tonkotsu Ramen

  • Tonkotsu Pork Broth (8 cups)
  • Good Quality Dried Ramen Noodles (12 ounces)
  • Large Or Extra Large Eggs (4 eggs)
  • Enoki Or Other Mushrooms (3 ounces)
  • Thinly Sliced Green Onions (2 stalks)

Chashu Pork Belly

  • Pork Belly (2 lbs)
  • Soy Sauce (1/4 cup)
  • Sake (1/2 cup)
  • Mirin (1/2 cup)
  • Sugar (1/4 cup)
  • Garlic (3 cloves)
  • Coarsely Chopped Green Onions (2 stalks)

Soy Bacon Tare

  • Good Quality Bacon (2 slices)
  • Soy Sauce (1/4 cup)
  • Mirin (2 tbps)
  • Sake (2 tbps)
  • Shiro Miso (4 tbps)

Miso Tare

  • Shiro Miso (1/2 cup)
  • Sake (1/4 cup)
  • Mirin (1/4 cup)
  • Kosher Salt (3/2 tsp)
  • Shichimi Togarashi (1 pinch, optional)

Instructions

Prep Ingredients

  1. Roll and tie the pork belly.
  2. Coarsely chop green onions for chasu pork belly.
  3. Slice green onions for the tonkotsu ramen.

Making The Chasu Pork Belly

  1. Combine the soy, sake, mirin, sugar, green onions, and pork belly in a ziploc bag.
  2. Stick a straw in the bag and seal the ziploc bag up against it such that the straw is the only opening.
  3. Sous vide the pork for 11 hours at 170F.
  4. After, remove only the pork from the bag and discard bag.
  5. Let the pork cool completely.
  6. Thin slice into 8 to 12 slices about 1/8 to 3/16 inches thick. Set aside.

Making The Soy Bacon Tare

  1. Combine all ingredients for soy bacon tare into a sauce pan.
  2. Let simmer at lowest setting for an hour. Add a little chicken stock if needed.
  3. After, remove the bacon.

Making The Miso Tare

  1. Combine all ingredients for the miso tare in a sauce pan.
  2. Simmer at lowest setting for 5 minutes.

Making The Medium Boiled Eggs

  1. Boil eggs for 7 minutes 30 seconds. Adjust if needed.
    Note: Egg should still be runny in the middle.
  2. After, submerge egg in cold water, peel, and cut in half to serve.

Making The Dish

  1. Boil ramen noodles as directed by packaging, or around 4 minutes.
  2. Put mushrooms in with noodles to cook with.
  3. Gently fry chasu pork in skillet until lightly browned.
  4. Pour 1/4 of total tare amount in a bowl for each serving.
  5. Pour 1/2 cup of tonkotsu broth in the bowl for each serving.
  6. Add the noodles, pour in another 3/2 cups of tonkosu broth in the bowl for each serving.
  7. Top with egg, mushrooms, pork, and green onions.

Important Notes

  • There are 2 different tare recipes. Use ONE or the other, not both, unless it is intended for 8 servings or more.
  • If using the miso tare, broth can be salted directly since it's easier than guessing how salty the tare should be.
  • Roasted pork shoulder could be used as a substitute for making the chasu pork.
